Leading the Capstone Project

In the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic, ensuring the health and safety of students and staff within educational institutions has become a top priority. As the leader of our capstone project, I had the privilege of guiding a talented team in developing the Sensory Signal Scanner (SSS) Health Monitoring System, a comprehensive solution designed to enhance health monitoring and safety protocols in schools.

The Sensory Signal Scanner (SSS) Health Monitoring System is an innovative project aimed at automating the process of temperature screening and health monitoring within educational institutions.

The system integrates hardware and software to detect elevated body temperatures, capture snapshots of individuals, store data in a centralized database, and send SMS alerts to school officials. This seamless integration ensures timely and effective responses to potential health threats, thereby maintaining a safe learning environment.

Leadership As the project leader, my role involved coordinating efforts, ensuring clear communication, and maintaining a cohesive vision for the project. I began by outlining a clear vision for the SSS Health Monitoring System. We held brainstorming sessions to define project goals, scope, and milestones. My priority was to ensure that every team member understood our objectives and felt confident in their roles.
